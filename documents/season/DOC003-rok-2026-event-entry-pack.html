How to Enter — Registration Process

Online Entry Portal

All entries must be submitted through the official ROK Cup South Africa online entry portal at rokthenats.co.za

  • Complete driver information
  • Class selection
  • Emergency contact details
  • Medical information

Entry Deadlines

Entries close 7 days before each event. Late entries may be accepted at the discretion of the organizer with a late fee surcharge.

  • National events: 7 days prior
  • Regional events: 7 days prior
  • Late entry fee: +50% of entry cost

Required Documents

All competitors must submit valid documentation with their entry:

  • Valid MSA competition licence
  • Medical certificate (current season)
  • Proof of age (for age-restricted classes)
  • Signed indemnity (parent/guardian if under 18)

Payment & Confirmation

Entry fee payment must be received to confirm entry:

  • Online payment via portal
  • EFT with reference number
  • Confirmation email sent upon receipt
  • Final entry list published 3 days before event
Entry Fees — 2026 Season
  • National NATS Weekend (Double-Header): Entry fees include both rounds, practice sessions, timing, and event administration. Exact fees published in event supplementary regulations.
  • Northern Regional Race (Single Day): Entry fees include practice, qualifying, heats, finals, timing and administration. Regional entry fees are typically lower than National events.
  • Pool Engine Rental: Separate rental fees apply for pool engine classes. Engine rental must be paid alongside entry fee to secure allocation.
  • Late Entry Surcharge: Entries received after the deadline are subject to a 50% surcharge if accepted by the organizer.

    • Important: Entry fees are non-refundable once the entry list has been published. Withdrawals after this point forfeit the entry fee. In cases of mechanical failure or medical issues preventing participation, fees may be credited to a future event at the organizer's discretion.

    Classes & Eligibility
  • Cadet (5–8 years): Tillotson Cadet engine, pool engine programme, entry-level class
  • Mini ROK U/10 (7–10 years): Vortex Mini ROK engine, pool engine programme, under 10 development class
  • Mini ROK (8–12 years): Vortex Mini ROK engine, pool engine programme, primary Mini category
  • OK-J (11–14 years): Vortex OK-J engine, FIA junior direct drive, pool engine programme
  • OK-N (14+ years): Vortex OK-N engine, FIA senior direct drive, pool engine programme
  • Academy: Development class, direct drive, pool engine programme, learning-focused environment

    • Age Eligibility: Driver age is determined as of 31 December 2026. Age ranges are guidelines; exact eligibility requirements are confirmed in the MSA ROK National Regulations 2026.

    Contact & Support

    Website: rokthenats.co.za — All event information, entries, results and documentation

    WhatsApp Groups: Join the official ROK Cup South Africa WhatsApp broadcast groups for race-day updates, bulletins and announcements. Links available on the website.

    Event Queries: For specific questions about entries, events or regulations, contact the organizer through the website contact form or event-specific communication channels published in advance of each event.
