Controlled Fuel

Mandatory fuel procedures for all ROK Cup SA events

‚õΩ
All Classes
Why Controlled Fuel?

ROK Cup South Africa operates a Controlled Fuel program to ensure:

  • Fair Competition: Every driver uses identical fuel, eliminating any performance advantage from fuel additives
  • Engine Protection: Approved fuel protects your engine investment
  • Cost Control: Prevents expensive "fuel wars" between competitors
  • International Alignment: Matches ROK Cup global standards
The Bottom Line Controlled fuel means your results come from driving skill and setup ‚Äî not from what's in your tank.
How The System Works

Step 1: Collection

Collect your fuel allocation from the Official Fuel Station during designated hours. Bring your own approved containers (Jerry cans).

Step 2: Mixing

Add your preferred 2-stroke oil to the fuel. The standard ratio is 4% (25:1) for all ROK engines. Some classes may specify different ratios ‚Äî check your class regulations.

Step 3: Use

Use ONLY the controlled fuel in your kart throughout the event. Any remaining fuel at the end of the event is yours to keep.

What to Bring
  • Clean, approved fuel containers (Jerry cans)
  • Your driver number / entry confirmation
  • Your own 2-stroke oil for mixing
  • Measuring jug for accurate oil mixing

Approved Containers

Use only proper fuel Jerry cans. No soft drink bottles, water bottles, or unapproved containers. Safety first!

Penalties for Non-Compliance
Zero Tolerance Policy ROK Cup South Africa operates a strict zero-tolerance policy on fuel irregularities.

Competitors found using non-controlled fuel will face:

  • First Offence: Immediate disqualification from the event
  • Second Offence: Season ban + loss of all championship points
  • Fuel Additives: Treated as fuel manipulation ‚Äî instant DQ

Random fuel testing may be conducted at any time during the event. Samples can be taken from your tank, fuel containers, or catch bottles.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I bring my own fuel from home?
A: No. All fuel must be collected on-site from the official fuel station.

Q: What if I run out during the event?
A: Additional fuel is available during top-up windows (see schedule above).

Q: Can I use any 2-stroke oil?
A: Yes, you can use your preferred 2-stroke oil brand for mixing.

Q: What octane is the controlled fuel?
A: ROK Cup SA uses 95 octane unleaded fuel, aligned with ROK international standards.
